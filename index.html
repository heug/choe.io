About

I’m Eugene, a software engineer based in Oakland. I love turning abstract ideas into useful applications that people can use to improve their quality of life. I believe in taking the time to do things the right way the first time. I operate on the principle that everyone has something to teach me. I live for the elusive moments of clarity and accomplishment that come with the completion of a job well done. I look forward to a future where an increasingly digital world allows people to spend more time doing the things they love.

Bio

I hold American, Canadian, and Korean passports, but really identify myself as a citizen of the world. Born in Toronto, I spent most of my formative years in the Bay Area. Fueled by a desire to explore an unknown city and an interest with working in a quantitative field, I left for New York University and graduated with a B.S. in Finance, after which I worked on trading floors for a few years—-long enough to know that I had zero interest in working in the world of finance.

I spent some time as a data analyst until I was laid off, and the combination of feeling like I was at a transitional point in life and the desire to have a career experiment which I found to be a great time to explore a serious hobby of mine and worked my way onto the line at a Michelin-starred restaurant.

Passion is great, but the desire for intellectual stimulation was too great for me to ignore.

Be it BASIC and HTML while in high school, VBA while in finance, or SQL and some Python while in software, I built my own bootstrapped tools to automate tasks and make everyone’s work more efficient. I started to teach myself to code in the free time I had during and after my chefing time, and ultimately enrolled in and completed the immersive program at Hack Reactor. Along the way, I’ve discovered a passion for building applications that I’m unable to shake even if I wanted to.

Bottom line: I love what I do and will do whatever it takes to further my career.

Let’s build something awesome together.
